Ribosome

Property Details
Size/Scale ~20-30 nm, ~2.5 MDa
Composition Bacterial: 50 proteins, 3 rRNAs; Eukaryotic: 80 proteins, 4 rRNAs
Interactions mRNA, tRNAs, translation factors, antibiotics
Community Size ~200 PIs, 1000-2000 students
Data Acquisition Methods X-ray crystallography, cryo-EM, biochemical assays
Nomenclature Ribosomal proteins named uL1 to uL44 (large subunit) and uS1 to uS31 (small subunit)
Relevant Loci Peptidyl Transferase Center (PTC), Decoding Center, Exit Tunnel

Main Vectors of Heterogeneity

Structural dynamics (e.g., ratcheting, rotation), compositional differences (e.g., bacterial vs. eukaryotic), evolutionary divergence, post-translational modifications

General Function

Translates mRNA into proteins, essential for protein synthesis

Key Research Groups

The Steitz Lab (Yale University), The Ramakrishnan Lab (MRC Laboratory of Molecular Biology), The Ban Lab (ETH Zurich), The Cate Lab (University of California, Berkeley)

Industry Applications

Antibiotics targeting bacterial ribosomes (e.g., macrolides, tetracyclines), cancer therapy targeting ribosome biogenesis, biotechnology for protein synthesis

Spliceosome

Property Details
Size/Scale ~20-40 nm, variable mass (dynamic)
Composition 5 snRNAs, ~100-200 proteins
Interactions Pre-mRNA, splicing factors, regulatory proteins
Community Size ~100-150 PIs, 500-1000 students
Data Acquisition Methods Cryo-EM, biochemical assays, genetic screens
Nomenclature snRNPs (U1, U2, U4, U5, U6), proteins named by function
PDB Structures 5LJN (human U1 snRNP), 6EX6 (human spliceosome)
Relevant Loci Active Site, Binding Sites for Exon-Intron Junctions

Main Vectors of Heterogeneity

Dynamic assembly states, species-specific differences, alternative splicing, structural flexibility

General Function

Removes introns from pre-mRNA, producing mature mRNA for translation

Key Research Groups

The Moore Lab (University of Massachusetts Medical School), The Staley Lab (University of California, San Francisco), The Shi Lab (Chinese Academy of Sciences), The Wilkinson Lab (Northwestern University)

Industry Applications

Drug development for myelodysplastic syndromes and cancers, gene editing for specific protein isoforms
